Fried Bangus: A Dish Overloaded With Flavors

Fried Bangus or Fried milkfish is a simple dish that is packed with flavors. The milkfish alone has its deliciously rich taste (especially the belly) while the marinade gives it a sour spicy flavor.

The marinade enhances the flavor of milk fish making it more appealing to the taste buds.

- Panlasang Pinoy

Ingredients:

  • 1 large boneless bangus milkfish: scales removed, cleaned and butterflied
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic: crushed
  • 1/2 teaspoon whole peppercorn
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup cooking oil

Preparation:

  1. Combine vinegar, garlic, whole peppercorn, and salt in a bowl. Stir.
  2. In a deep wide plate, pour-in the mixture and then lay down the fish in a manner that the sliced part is facing down (skin should face up). Cover and place in the fridge. Marinate for 8 to 12 hours.
  3. Heat the cooking oil in a pan. Fry both sides of the bangus in medium heat until the color turns medium brown (note: it should take around 5 minutes per side).
  4. Note: the oil has a tendency to splatter because of the liquid from the marinade. Make sure to cover the pan while leaving open a small space for the steam to escape.
  5. Serve with spiced vinegar (sinamak).

Cooking Tips:

  • 8-12 hours marinating time is key for maximum flavor.
  • 5 minutes per side frying achieves perfect medium brown color.
